Cybersecurity is not something you absorb by skimming slides and memorizing commands.

It is learned the hard way — by enumerating what others overlook, tracing failure back to its cause, exploiting weak assumptions, and understanding **why** something works before trusting **how** to use it.

That is the work I care about.

I build and teach hands-on security experiences through Capture the Flag competitions, custom challenge design, lab environments, and scenario-based training that push learners beyond surface-level knowledge and into real technical understanding. My focus is practical offensive security: the kind that forces you to think, adapt, troubleshoot, and earn the result.

This site is where all of that lives.

It is my archive for CTF write-ups, challenge breakdowns, lab notes, build logs, and technical resources created to help students, competitors, and curious builders sharpen their skills. Some of it is instructional. Some of it is experimental. All of it is grounded in the same idea:

> If you do not understand the **why**, the **how** will fail you when it matters.

I am especially interested in building learning paths that take people from curiosity to capability — not by watering things down, but by making them approachable without stripping away the technical depth. Good training should not just hand someone a solution. It should teach them how to see the path for themselves.

So that is what you will find here:

- [`CTF write-ups`](/writeups/) that explain both the process and the reasoning
- [`custom challenges`](/challenges/) built to teach more than a single trick
- [`lab walkthroughs`](/labs/) covering enumeration, exploitation, escalation, and pivoting
- [`training material`](/resources/) designed for students who want to move beyond theory
- [`notes and resources`](/resources/) for people who care enough to dig deeper

## Philosophy

The point is not to memorize commands.

The point is to become the kind of person who can land in an unfamiliar environment, observe carefully, ask the right questions, test the right assumptions, and keep moving until the system gives up its truth.

That mindset matters more than any individual tool.

And that is what this site is built to support.
