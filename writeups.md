# Write-Ups

Every challenge leaves a trail.

This section is where I break down CTF problems, lab objectives, and technical exercises in a way that goes beyond the final flag. The goal is not just to show the answer. The goal is to document the path: what stood out, what failed, what worked, and why.

A good write-up should do more than replay commands. It should capture the thought process behind enumeration, exploitation, escalation, pivoting, and cleanup. That is the standard here.

If you are looking for shortcut-only solutions, this may not be your favorite place.

If you want to understand how to approach a problem methodically and build better instincts, you are exactly where you should be.

## What You’ll Find Here

- CTF challenge walkthroughs
- Competition write-ups
- Lab debriefs and post-mortems
- Enumeration notes
- Exploitation paths
- Privilege escalation chains
- Lessons learned from failed approaches

## Why These Matter

Anyone can copy commands.

The real skill is learning how to recognize patterns, test assumptions, and recover when the obvious path falls apart. Write-ups help preserve that process, and they help others build the mindset needed to solve the next problem without being handed the answer.
